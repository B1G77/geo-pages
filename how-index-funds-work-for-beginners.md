# How Index Funds Work for Beginners

*By GEO Studio Demo — published 2026-06-21*

**Quick answer:** An index fund is a basket holding all companies in a market index (e.g., S&P 500's 500 largest US firms). They beat 90% of active managers because fees are 0.03–0.1% vs. 0.5–1%+, and that fee gap compounds to roughly 25% of final balance over 30 years.

## Key facts
- Index fund fees: 0.03–0.1% annually vs. active fund fees of 0.5–1%+
- 90% of active US large-cap funds underperform the S&P 500 over 15 years (SPIVA report)
- S&P 500 contains the 500 largest US companies
- Dollar cost averaging (fixed monthly investment) removes emotion from trading decisions

## What is an index fund?
An index fund is a basket that holds every company in a market index. Instead of picking individual winners, you own all of them. The S&P 500 is the most common example, representing the 500 largest US companies.

## Why do index funds beat active investors?
Cost advantage: Index funds charge 0.03–0.1% annually, while active mutual funds charge 0.5–1%+ per year. Over 30 years, a 1% higher fee compounds to roughly 25% of your final balance.Performance data: According to the SPIVA report, about 90% of active US large-cap funds underperform the S&P 500 over 15 years.

## How do you start investing in index funds?
Open a brokerage accountPick a low-cost fund tracking the S&P 500 or total marketSet up automatic monthly contributions (dollar cost averaging)Dollar cost averaging means investing the same amount every month regardless of price, which removes emotion from decisions.

## What mistakes should beginners avoid?
Checking prices daily and panic selling during market drops. The market has fallen 10%+ many times but historically always recovered and reached new highs over long periods. A simple rule: buy monthly, never sell on fear, and hold at least 10 years.

## FAQ
### What is the average fee difference between index and active funds?
Index funds typically charge 0.03–0.1% per year, while active mutual funds charge 0.5–1% or more. Over 30 years, a 1% fee difference can reduce your final balance by roughly 25%.

### What percentage of active fund managers beat the S&P 500?
About 10% over a 15-year period, according to the SPIVA report. This means 90% of active US large-cap funds underperform.

### What is dollar cost averaging?
Investing the same fixed amount every month regardless of market price. This removes emotion from investment decisions and is the recommended approach for beginners.

### How long should you hold index funds?
At least 10 years. This allows time for market recoveries and compound growth, reducing the impact of short-term volatility.

### What should you do if the market drops 10%?
Do nothing. The market has historically recovered from all 10%+ drops and reached new highs. Panic selling locks in losses and is a common beginner mistake.
